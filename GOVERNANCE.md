# Governance

Open Fiction Corpus is maintained through public issues, pull requests, and versioned releases.

## Decision principles

Maintainers prioritise:

1. legal and provenance clarity;
2. reproducibility;
3. corpus quality;
4. transparent classifications;
5. usefulness across models and training frameworks.

Rights uncertainty blocks release. Popularity or usefulness does not override that rule.

## Changes

Routine metadata, tooling, and documentation changes use ordinary pull-request review. Changes to schemas, rights policy, canonical cleaning rules, or release composition should include a public rationale and migration notes when existing records are affected.

## Disputes

Genre classification is editorial and may reasonably be disputed. Rights and provenance claims require evidence. Maintainers may exclude a work while a dispute remains unresolved without declaring a final legal conclusion.

## Releases

Each dataset release must identify the exact catalogue commit, build-tool version, included work IDs, checksums, and pack definitions used to create it.
