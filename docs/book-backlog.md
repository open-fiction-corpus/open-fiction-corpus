# Book Backlog

This file tracks books considered for the Open Fiction Corpus. It is an editorial planning document, not a rights determination and not a guarantee that a work will enter a release.

A work is considered **added** only when:

1. its rights and edition have been reviewed;
2. its manifest exists under `catalog/works/`;
3. its cleaned text has passed validation and human review; and
4. it has appeared in a published dataset release.

## Status legend

- **Candidate** — worth considering; source and rights not yet checked
- **Rights review** — exact edition, translator, and jurisdictional status being checked
- **Ready** — approved source and edition identified; ready for processing
- **In progress** — manifest, extraction, cleaning, or review underway
- **Added** — included in a published dataset release
- **Deferred** — useful, but intentionally postponed

## Priority legend

- **P0** — first-release core
- **P1** — strong next-wave candidate
- **P2** — useful later expansion
- **Heritage** — historically important, but should live primarily in a separate foundational-classics pack

## Foundational classics and premodern literature

These works are valuable, but they should not dominate the main prose corpus. Most are poetry, premodern language, or translations. Their primary home should be an optional `foundational-classics` or `heritage` pack.

A sensible initial ceiling would be:

- **0–2%** of the balanced general-fiction pack;
- **5–10%** of a broad historical-literature pack; and
- unrestricted inclusion only in a dedicated heritage pack.

## Corpus-balance guardrails

Before promoting candidates into a balanced release, check that the pack does not become dominated by:

- one author or one series;
- Victorian prose;
- male authors;
- British and US literature alone;
- omniscient narration;
- novels at the expense of complete short narratives;
- children's fiction;
- poetry or translated epics;
- gothic and speculative fiction at the expense of realism and comedy.

Suggested defaults for the general-fiction pack:

- no more than **two works per author**;
- at least **35% women-authored works** in early releases, with the target rising as the catalogue expands;
- at least **20% short-story collections or individually catalogued short stories**;
- no more than **10% children's fiction**;
- no more than **2% foundational or premodern works**;
- translations excluded until the translation-specific rights workflow is tested.

## How to update this list

- Change the status when work begins or a decision is made.
- Add the exact edition or translation in the notes before moving a work to **Ready**.
- Link the merged manifest when moving a work to **In progress**.
- Record the first dataset version containing the work when moving it to **Added**.
- Explain why a work is **Deferred** so future contributors do not repeat the same investigation.
