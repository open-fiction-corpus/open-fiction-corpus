# Data licensing and rights

The MIT licence in this repository applies to the project code and documentation. It does **not** automatically apply to literary works, editions, translations, introductions, illustrations, or other source material represented by the catalogue.

Every work manifest must record:

- the upstream source and pinned revision or edition;
- the author and any translator, editor, or other rights-relevant contributor;
- the asserted rights status;
- the jurisdictions that have actually been reviewed;
- the evidence supporting that status;
- any restrictions or unresolved questions.

A work may enter a public dataset release only when its redistribution basis is documented and accepted under the project's rights policy.

Public-domain status is jurisdiction-specific. A work that is public domain in the United States may still be protected elsewhere. Translations and edited editions can have independent copyright even when the original work is public domain.

Contributor-owned contemporary fiction requires an explicit licence permitting redistribution and machine-learning use. Submission alone is not evidence that the submitter owns the necessary rights.

The project makes provenance and review information available but does not provide legal advice or guarantee that a work is unrestricted in every jurisdiction. Dataset users remain responsible for evaluating their own use and location.
